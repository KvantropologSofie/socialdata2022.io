Conclusion

Through this project we have investigated differences in exposure to air pollution both globally and nation-wise. In line with the existing research, the data and visualizations show that countries with lower GDP are more affected by bad air quality. Thus, our findings indicate that there exists environmental inequality on a global scale. High concentrations of PM(2.5) are especially prevalent across Asia and Africa where many countries at the same time are suffering from high death rates caused by pollution.

We further zoomed in on Denmark and found huge differences in both income and exposure to air pollutants. Through the Danish part of the project, it was not possible to find clear signs of environmental inequality, as both municipalities with higher and lower income are exposed to the same high concentrations of air pollution. However, these findings indicate that it is essential to assess each respective country on an individual and more granular level in order to capture variations and nuances within.
