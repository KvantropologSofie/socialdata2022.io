Exploring Air Pollution Globally


Air pollution is not distributed equally across the globe. Some countries are exposed to significantly higher concentrations of air pollution than others, and unfortunately, these countries are often the ones with less economic resources.

In the first part of the website, we set out to explore how air pollution is distributed globally throughout time. Based on data and visualizations we find that less resourceful countries are suffering the most from negative health consequences of air pollution.

Data for the global analysis #

The data used to analyse social inequality and pollution globally is combined from the following three sources. It contains information about the annual concentrations of PM(2.5), death rates related to pollution and features related to the economy and health of all countries.

WHO

The pollution data used in the analysis is retrieved from WHO and contains concentrations of ambient air pollution for almost all countries globally. The dataset is only covering one type of pollution, PM(2.5), which will be our main pollutant throughout all parts of the project.

Our world in data

We are further retrieving a range of datasets from Our World in Data regarding death rates related to the exposure of PM(2.5), the cardiovascular disease death rate, and the health expenditure as % of GDP. All datasets from OWID originates from WHO.
