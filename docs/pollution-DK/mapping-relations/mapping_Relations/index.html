Relating Inequality and Pollution in Denmark

Scatter plots show the dynamics of air pollution and socioeconomic factors
On this page we will dig further into the dynamics of air pollution and socioeconomic factors. Based on the plots, there are no clear signs of environmental inequality in a Danish context. Instead we observe that individuals living in municipalities with higher economic capital can expect to live longer, but also that both individuals from the municipalities with the lowest (Lolland) and highest (Rudersdal and Gentofte), are experiencing the same high concentrations of pollution.

The scatterplots included in this section are interactive, and you can zoom or hover over the points to obtain information about the respective municipalities. Moreover, the color of each point corresponds to the Danish region to which the municipality belongs.

Figure A shows the relation between income and air pollution. Through the plot it is evident that the points are divided into two groups; municipalities in Zealand and in Jutland, where municipalities in Zealand are experiencing higher concentrations of air pollution. Furthermore, municipalities in this area have larger variance in income, ranging from Bornholm with an average income of 262.203, to Gentofte with an average annual income of 611.952. This means that individuals with different social backgrounds are exposed to the same high concentrations of air pollution. Thus, there is no clear relationship between income and air pollution in a Danish context.

Plot B shows the relationship between life expectancy and pollution. The points are again clustered in two groups, Eastern and Western municipalities, but there is again no relationship between life expectancy and pollution levels. Both municipalities with the lowest life expectancy (Lolland) and the highest life expectancy (Rudersdal and Gentofe) are experiencing the same concentrations of air pollution.

Plot C shows the relationship between life expectancy and income. Through this plot we find a strong relationship between the factors, where municipalities with higher incomes, also have a higher life expectancy. However, this type of social inequality does not appear to be related to pollution levels.
