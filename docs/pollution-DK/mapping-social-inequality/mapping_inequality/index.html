Mapping Social Inequality in Denmark

There are differences in income and health across Danish municipalities
In order to investigate whether there is environmental inequality in a Danish context we further map income distribution, the life expectancy of children born in 2015 and the average health expenses spent on each citizen across the Danish municipalities. By ticking off the boxes in the legend it is possible to look into the different factors and explore social inequality in Denmark. It is further possible to zoom in, to explore the nuances in the distribution of social and monetary resources.

Based on the map it is evident that the Capital Region of Denmark has higher annual income than the remaining regions. In the map of social factors, we see that the municipalities with lower incomes and higher health expenditure are the places where children born today can expect to have shorter lives.
